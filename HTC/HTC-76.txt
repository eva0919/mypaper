<title:>Asian-Pacific Stocks
Taiwan Gets Boost From Tech Shares
<Date:>28 August 2014
<Content:>
Gains in technology stocks helped the Taiwan market outperform, while most Asian markets were up amid hopes that the European Central Bank will introduce new stimulus measures.
Improved optimism about the global economy and anticipation of new product launches, including the latest iteration of Apple's iPhone expected next month, fueled bargain hunting on Wednesday in Taiwan, where the Taiex index rose 1% to 9485.59.
Hon Hai Precision Industry, one of Apple's major assemblers, was up 1.8%, smartphone provider HTC rose 1.5% and touch-panel maker TPK Holding jumped 5.4%.
Gains were more muted on other markets, albeit buoyed by a climb in the U.S. market overnight, as well as indications from the European Central Bank that it is considering bond buying.
The S&P 500 closed above 2000 for the first time Tuesday in New York. In the euro zone, investors have seen a marked deterioration of economic data from the euro zone in recent weeks, but they are betting that Europe may be edging closer to a large-scale asset-purchase program known as quantitative easing, which has lifted euro-zone bonds.
Japan's Nikkei Stock Average rose 0.1% to 15534.82, Singapore's Straits Times Index rose 0.6% to 3341.46 and South Korea's Kospi was up 0.3% at 2074.93. India's Sensex rose 0.4% to 26560.15, gaining for a fifth consecutive session to end at another record.
Australia's S&P/ASX 200 edged up 0.2% to 5651.20, as investors appeared more cautious after the index's gains in the past two weeks.
The region's worst performer was Hong Kong, where the Hang Seng Index fell 0.6% to 24918.75, as disappointing bank profits pulled down the financial sector.
Agricultural Bank of China, China's third-largest lender by assets, lost 0.8% after the bank reported the slowest growth in first-half profits in four years, mainly due to recent government scrutiny on lending-related fees. Shares in China Construction Bank fell 1.2%.
In corporate news, Otsuka Holdings rose 7.5% in Tokyo after announcing a midterm business plan in which it targets an operating profit of 200 billion yen for the fiscal year ending December 2018. The firm also set its dividend payout target at 100 yen a share, above the consensus view.
Boral climbed 4.5% in Sydney after signaling an increase in demand for building materials.
